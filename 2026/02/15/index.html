Macro Thesis: The "June Pivot" & The Bond Bull Case

The market has decisively shifted its expectations regarding Federal Reserve policy. Prediction markets now price a 92.5% probability of a pause at the March meeting, effectively killing the "March Cut" narrative. However, the probability of a rate cut by the June meeting has risen to ~75%.

  • Rates & Bonds: Despite the "higher for longer" front-end narrative, the long end of the curve is bidding. FRED data shows the 10Y Treasury yield compressing from 4.29% earlier in the month to 4.09% as of Feb 12. This divergence suggests the bond market is front-running a slowdown or pricing in a geopolitical safety premium.
    • Actionable: Duration exposure is becoming attractive. The risk/reward for long-term treasuries is improving as yields slide despite sticky short-term policy expectations.
  • Inflation: Markets are pricing February annual inflation to come in around 2.4%. This steady but elevated level supports the Fed's pause but leaves the door open for Q2 easing if labor softens further (unemployment currently steady at 4.3%).

Geopolitics & Energy: The Iran Risk Premium

The most significant asymmetric risk currently lies in the energy sector. The convergence of the Trump/Netanyahu agreement to squeeze Iranian oil and prediction markets showing a ~45-50% probability of a US or Israel strike on Iran by June 30, 2026, creates a bullish setup for oil.

  • Second Order Effects:
    • Oil Supply: Any kinetic action or strict enforcement of sanctions removes supply from the market. With WTI hovering in the mid-$60s, the geopolitical risk premium is arguably underpriced.
    • Defense: Continued conflict in Ukraine (Russia making tactical gains in Pokrovsk) and rising Middle East tensions reinforce the long-term bull case for defense primes and aerospace.
    • China: If Iranian oil to China is cut off, China must bid for supply elsewhere, potentially driving up global benchmarks.
